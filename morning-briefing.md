# ☀️ The Daily Morning Briefing

*A little gift from me to you.*

I used to open five different apps every morning just to figure out what my day looked like. Now I paste one thing into Claude and get the whole picture in about 30 seconds: what happened yesterday, what's on today, and the few things that actually need me.

Honestly, it's the one habit that made my mornings feel calmer. Here's everything you need to steal it.

## What you'll need

Just the Claude app (desktop or mobile), or [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) in a browser.

If you connect your email and calendar to Claude, the briefing gets a lot smarter, because it can pull your real meetings and inbox. If you haven't connected them yet, no big deal. Claude will just ask what's going on and build the briefing from your answer.

Not sure if your email and calendar are connected? Just try Step 1. If Claude asks you what's on your calendar, that's your sign they aren't hooked up yet, and that's completely fine.

## Step 1: Get your first briefing

Open Claude, copy the whole box below, paste it in, and hit send.

```
You're my morning briefing assistant. Give me a clean, scannable briefing for today. Keep it tight and skip the fluff.

Pull from my connected email and calendar if you have access. If you don't, just ask me what's on my plate and build the briefing from my answer.

Format it exactly like this:

☀️ GOOD MORNING, [today's date]

📌 YESTERDAY
- 2 to 4 bullets on what actually mattered (key emails, meetings, anything that moved)

📅 TODAY
- My schedule in time order
- Flag anything I need to prep for before it starts

✅ NEEDS ME
- The 3 to 5 things genuinely worth my attention today, most important first
- Call out any buried or time-sensitive emails I might miss

🎯 ONE THING
- If I only get one thing done today, this is it

Rules: be direct, prioritize hard, and leave out anything that doesn't matter. If nothing is urgent, just say "nothing on fire, here's a clean day."
```

That's it. You've got a briefing on demand any time you want one.

## What you'll get back

Something like this:

> **☀️ GOOD MORNING, Monday, July 6**
>
> **📌 YESTERDAY**
> - Closed the loop with Sam on the venue, the deposit is paid
> - Heard back from the accountant. She needs your signature this week
>
> **📅 TODAY**
> - 10:00 Team standup (nothing to prep)
> - 1:30 Dentist on 2nd & Main, leave by 1:10
> - 4:00 Call with Jordan. He asked for the budget beforehand, so have it ready
>
> **✅ NEEDS ME**
> 1. Sign the accountant's form (it's blocking your filing)
> 2. Send Jordan the budget before 4:00
> 3. Reply to Mom, buried under Friday's newsletters
>
> **🎯 ONE THING**
> Sign that form. Everything else can wait a day. This one can't.

Clean, honest, and actually useful. No 20-item to-do list, just the stuff that matters.

## Step 2: Make it show up on its own (optional)

If you'd rather not paste it every morning, ask Claude to run it for you:

```
Set up a scheduled task that runs my morning briefing prompt every day
at 7:00 AM and send me the result.
```

Pick whatever time you actually roll out of bed. Claude handles the rest. (Scheduling lives in the Claude desktop app.)

## Make it yours

The prompt is just a starting point. The more Claude knows about your life, the sharper it gets. A few easy tweaks:

- **Flag your people:** "Always call out anything from my partner, my boss, or my kid's school."
- **Add a nudge to move:** "Include a MOVE line reminding me to work out or take a walk."
- **Set the vibe:** "Keep it warm and encouraging," or "Just the facts, skip the pep talk."
- **Give it context:** tell Claude what projects you're juggling and who matters most. It starts connecting the dots on its own.
- **End on a high note:** "Finish with one small thing I can look forward to today."

Change anything. Break anything. It's yours now.

## A few quick questions

**Do I need to pay for Claude?**
A free account works for the paste-and-run version. Scheduling it to run on its own needs the desktop app.

**Is my email and calendar data safe?**
Claude only reads what you connect, and only when you ask. Nothing gets posted or sent anywhere without you.

**It asked what's on my calendar instead of just knowing. Why?**
Your calendar isn't connected yet. Either connect it in Claude's settings, or just answer the question. The briefing works either way.

**Can I do this for the evening too?**
Yep. Copy the prompt, swap "morning" for "evening," and ask it to preview tomorrow instead of recapping yesterday.
